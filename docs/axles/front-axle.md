# OM617TJ Front Axle

## Overview

The front axle setup for the OM617TJ is built around a heavily modified Dana 44 sourced from a 2006 Jeep Wrangler LJ. The axle has been reinforced and upgraded for high-strength off-road use, larger tires, hydro assist steering, and improved braking performance.

This setup combines OEM Jeep components with aftermarket upgrades focused on durability, serviceability, steering strength, and braking performance.

# Axle Reinforcement

## Artec Industries Axle Truss

The axle housing is reinforced using an Artec Industries truss system.

### Purpose
- Prevent axle tube bending
- Increase housing rigidity
- Improve suspension mount strength
- Reduce flex under large tire load and locker engagement

### Notes
- Fully welded truss system
- Designed for long-arm suspension stress
- Helps protect ring and pinion alignment under load

---

## Barnes 4WD Inner Axle Sleeves

Inner axle sleeves were installed inside the factory axle tubes.

### Purpose
- Reinforce factory axle tubes internally
- Reduce risk of tube bending
- Increase resistance to hard impacts

### Notes
- Welded installation
- Works in conjunction with the axle truss for maximum rigidity

---

## Artec Industries C-Gussets

Artec C-gussets were added to reinforce the inner knuckle Cs.

### Purpose
- Prevent inner C bending
- Maintain alignment during hard impacts
- Strengthen steering and suspension mounting area

### Notes
- Common upgrade for TJ/LJ Dana 44 axles running larger tires
- Especially important with hydro assist steering and beadlocks

# Suspension & Steering Integration

This axle is designed to integrate with:

- Hydro assist steering
- Long-arm suspension
- 37-inch tires
- Beadlock wheels
- Front selectable locker
- Heavy off-road use

The combination of reinforced housing components, RCV shafts, locking hubs, and upgraded brakes creates a highly durable front axle assembly capable of handling aggressive off-road driving and high torque loads from the OM617 diesel engine.
